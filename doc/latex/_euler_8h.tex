\subsection{Detailed Description}
\mbox{\hyperlink{_euler_8h}{Euler.\+h}} contains several functions, for operations of quaternions, converting between Euler angles, rotation matrices and unit quaternions and sampling rotation matrices from even distribution. 

Quaternions are a number system that extends the complex numbers. Unit quaternions provide a convenient mathematical notation for representing rotations of objects in 3D. Compared to Euler angles, they are simpler to compose and aovid the problem of glimbal lock. Compared to rotation matrices, they are more compact and more efficient. Moroever, unlike Euler angles, unit quaternions do not rely on the choosing and order of the rotation axes.

To be noticed, Euler angles in this file follow the standard of Z\+XZ Euler system. In other words, Euler angle set $\left\{\phi, \theta, \psi\right\}$ stands for rotating along Z axis with $\phi$, followed by rotating along X axis with $\theta$, and followed by rotating along Z axis with $\psi$.

Calculate the product of two quaternions $\mathbf{q_1}$ and $\mathbf{q_2}$. 

Assuming that $\mathbf{q_1} = \left(w_1, x_1, y_1, z_1\right)$ and $\mathbf{q_2} = \left(w_2, x_2, y_2, z_2\right)$, the product can be calculated as \[ \begin{pmatrix} w_1 \\ x_1 \\ y_1 \\ z_1 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} w_2 \\ x_2 \\ y_2 \\ z_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} w_{1}w_{2} - x_{1}x_{2} - y_{1}y_{2} - z_{1}z_{2} \\ w_{1}x_{2} + x_{1}w_{2} + y_{1}z_{2} - z_{1}y_{2} \\ w_{1}y_{2} - x_{1}z_{2} + y_{1}w_{2} + z_{1}x_{2} \\ w_{1}z_{2} + x_{1}y_{2} - y_{1}x_{2} + z_{1}w_{2} \end{pmatrix} \]
